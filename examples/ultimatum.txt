Ultimatum Bargaining

6 agents are paired into 3 proposer-responder pairs each round. Pairs are randomly assigned. A pot of 100 units is available per pair.

Each round, the proposer offers a split by choosing an amount (0 to 100) to give to the responder. The responder simultaneously sets a minimum acceptable offer (0 to 100). If the proposer's offer meets or exceeds the responder's minimum, the deal is accepted: the responder gets the offered amount and the proposer keeps the rest. If the offer is below the minimum, both get nothing for that round.

Roles alternate each round: proposers become responders and vice versa.

If total payouts across all pairs drop below 50 for 5 consecutive rounds, the market has collapsed.

Run for 40 rounds. Each agent can observe their own accumulated wealth and the average acceptance rate from the previous round. They cannot see other agents' individual strategies or thresholds.
